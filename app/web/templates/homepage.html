Why It Feels Grounded

Built for careful batches, not mass outreach.

The product works more like a research notebook and sending desk than a generic growth dashboard.

Research context first

Every lead starts with a real faculty trail.

Search across academic sources, store what you found, and keep notes on recent ML papers, benchmarks, or systems work before a draft is ever generated.

Reviewable writing

The message queue is designed for editing, scoring, and judgment.

Drafts are checked for specificity, authenticity, overlap, and completeness so an ML outreach note can reference the right lab context instead of sounding generic.

Controlled delivery

Sending is intentionally conservative.

Rate limits, suppression checks, draft mode, and delivery logging keep the workflow credible when the stakes are real.

Workflow

A realistic outreach cycle, from shortlist to sent folder.

1

Search faculty by topic, field, and institution.

Use the finder to build a small list of plausible contacts for areas like interpretability, RL, multimodal models, or ML systems rather than a giant unvetted spreadsheet.

2

Attach research context and notes.

Keep factual summaries, recent work, and your own angle so each future draft has substance behind it, whether the lab works on vision, NLP, safety, or theory.

3

Generate a draft, then score it like an editor would.

Specificity, relevance, completeness, and similarity checks make weak outreach obvious before it reaches an inbox.

4

Approve only what you would actually send.

Edit, reject, or hold drafts until the tone, ask, and research references feel believable.

5

Deliver through a controlled queue.

Use SMTP or draft-based sending with suppression checks and session-level oversight so delivery stays measured.
